For your 80th birthday
Summer 2026

Dear Mimi,

First of all, happy birthday! So many great memories that it's hard to pick just one. When I picture you, the first thing I think of is summer. Every year growing up, Elle, Aaron, and I would fly out and spend a few weeks with you and Grandpa, and you'd pack more into those weeks than felt possible: Six Flags Magic Kingdom, Lion King in San Francisco, dinner at Farallon (more than once), staying with you at L'Auberge in Carmel, swimming, tennis, games, Ghirardelli Square, In-N-Out. There were honestly too many to list.

And the little things, like those Kellogg's Variety Pack boxes and chocolate pudding you always kept around. Getting to pick your own box felt like a big deal as a kid, and Elle, Aaron, and I would get so excited choosing our favorites.

I also think about the skate park mornings. You and Grandpa would get us there early, before any of the other kids showed up, so we could practice without an audience. We were pretty shy back then, but you both just knew and worked around it.

I didn't think much about the effort behind any of that back then, the big trips or the quiet stuff like the early mornings. Now that Melissa and I have Ava and Micah of our own, some mornings just getting both of them fed and out the door leaves me wiped out. So I think about you and Grandpa hosting a whole pack of grandkids for weeks at a time, and I genuinely don't know how you did it without collapsing by 8pm every night.

What I understand now is that it wasn't just fun for us. You were building something, on purpose, every summer. And you're doing the exact same thing right now with Ava and Micah. They already light up when they see you, exactly the way I did.

I feel lucky every day that we live close enough to see you as much as we do. Thank you for all of it, then and now.

I love you so much, and happy 80th birthday, Mimi. Here's to many more summers together.

Alex
